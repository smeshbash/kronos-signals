# **9. Funding Rate Framework**

Funding rates on perpetual futures are paid every 8 hours. The direction of the rate determines whether a position is paying or receiving funding. This is bidirectional and must be treated as both a cost and a revenue component.

## **9.1 Directional Funding Rate Matrix**

| **Position Direction** | **Funding Rate Direction** | **Financial Impact** | **System Action** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Long | Positive (longs pay shorts) | Cost to position | Exit if cost exceeds 0.1% per 8H |
| Long | Negative (shorts pay longs) | Revenue to position | Factor positively into P&L — extends viability |
| Short | Negative (shorts pay longs) | Cost to position | Exit if cost exceeds 0.1% per 8H |
| Short | Positive (longs pay shorts) | Revenue to position | Factor positively into P&L — extends viability |

## **9.2 Funding Rate as Market Signal**

| **Funding Rate Reading** | **Market Interpretation** | **Risk Check Action** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Extreme positive (above +0.3% per 8H) | Overleveraged longs — potential long squeeze risk | Flag caution on new long entries — **raise** effective confidence threshold by 10% relative (e.g. 0.60 → 0.66). "Reduce" in earlier draft was a drafting error; correct intent is a stricter filter. Module 5 implements this as `effective = threshold × 1.10`. |
| Moderate positive (0.1% to 0.3%) | Bullish sentiment, elevated but not extreme | Normal processing — cost factored in |
| Near zero (-0.05% to +0.05%) | Balanced market | No restriction |
| Moderate negative (-0.1% to -0.3%) | Bearish sentiment, elevated but not extreme | Normal processing — revenue factored in |
| Extreme negative (below -0.3% per 8H) | Overleveraged shorts — potential short squeeze risk | Flag caution on new short entries — **raise** effective confidence threshold by 10% relative. Same rule as extreme positive, applied to short positions. |

## **9.3 Funding Rate Tax Treatment**

| **Item** | **Tax Treatment** | **Tracker Action** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Funding received | Taxable income at 30% | Logged separately in Tax & TDS Tracker — included in gross income |
| Funding paid | Cost of acquisition — deductible | Offset against trade P&L before tax calculation |
| Net funding position | Reported in Schedule VDA | Monthly and annual summary generated |

## **9.4 Funding Rate Data Availability Constraint**

Delta Exchange settles funding at 00:00, 08:00, and 16:00 UTC. Module 1 fetches the settled rate at 00:02, 08:02, and 16:02 UTC (+2 min buffer). **All downstream modules that read from the `funding_rates` table are subject to this constraint:** the newly settled rate is not present in the database until Module 1's cron job completes at :02. Modules 5 (Risk Check), 7 (Position Monitor), 8 (Portfolio Manager), and 9 (Tax & TDS Tracker) must not query `funding_rates` at the exact settlement time (00:00, 08:00, 16:00 UTC) expecting the new rate to be there — it will not be. Any logic that needs the most recent settled rate should execute after :02 past the settlement hour, or read the most recent available row and accept it may be from the previous period if queried before Module 1 has run.

# **12. Slippage Model**

The slippage model is built entirely from self-collected Delta Exchange API data. No paid third-party data source is required. The model is not active during pre-live phase — it is calibrated from the data collected during that period.

## **12.1 Data Collection**

| **Data Type** | **Source** | **Frequency** | **Purpose** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| OHLCV candle data | Delta Exchange REST API (via CCXT) | Cron-aligned to 4H candle close: 00:02, 04:02, 08:02, 12:02, 16:02, 20:02 UTC. +2 min buffer ensures candle is finalised on exchange before fetch. | Signal generation and slippage context |
| Order book snapshots (L1) | Delta Exchange WebSocket (v2/ticker channel) | Every 15 minutes | Bid-ask spread, mark price, open interest, and funding rate — all sourced from the same v2/ticker message. Stored in orderbook_snapshots. |
| Trade execution fills | Delta Exchange WebSocket (all_trades channel) | Real-time stream; aggregated into 15-min windows at flush | Stored as event_type='fill_window' rows in events table: VWAP, fill count, taker_buy_ratio, price range per symbol per 15-min window. Used by Slippage Model for slippage distribution analysis (Section 12.3). Raw ticks not retained — 15-min aggregation is sufficient for swing trading slippage estimation. |
| Funding rates | Delta Exchange REST API (/v2/tickers endpoint) | Cron-aligned to 8H settlement windows: 00:02, 08:02, 16:02 UTC. +2 min buffer ensures settled rate is captured. | Funding rate cost/revenue tracking |
| Open interest | Delta Exchange WebSocket (v2/ticker — same subscription as order book snapshots) | Every 15 minutes via orderbook_snapshots table | Co-located with bid/ask/spread in orderbook_snapshots. ## **12.3 Slippage Estimation Logic**

| **Input** | **Output** | **Usage** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Current bid-ask spread from order book snapshot | Estimated slippage in basis points | Risk check module: is spread acceptable for limit entry? **Implementation note:** Module 5 attaches the slippage estimate to RiskCheckResult as an informational field — no spec-defined rejection threshold exists for slippage bps alone. Extreme spread is gated separately by the exchange circuit breaker check (Section 8.4: spread > 1% of mark price). Module 6 (Execution) uses the attached estimate when deciding limit order placement and fill probability. |
| Order book depth at entry price level | Fill probability at limit price | Execution module: confidence of fill within 4H window |
| Historical fill data vs mid-price | Slippage distribution per asset per market condition | P&L forecasting: expected vs actual execution cost. **Data model constraint:** Fill data is available exclusively as `fill_window` aggregated rows in the `events` table (event_type='fill_window'). Raw tick-level fill data is never stored — the aggregation is one-way and irreversible. Module 2 must query fill_window events and work from VWAP distributions, taker_buy_ratio, fill count, and price range per 15-min window. Any Module 2 design that assumes tick-level granularity will fail at query time. |
| Funding rate and open interest context | Liquidity regime classifier (high/normal/low) | Adjusts slippage estimate for current market state |

**Note: **A static slippage assumption was explicitly rejected during requirements design. Crypto slippage is regime-dependent — it is lowest during calm conditions and highest during volatility, which is precisely when Kronos generates its strongest signals. A static assumption would either over-penalise good trades or under-estimate risk during volatile entries.

| **Step** | **Calculation** | **Example (at 4% gross monthly return)** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Gross monthly profit | Total P&L before all deductions | ₹4,000 |
| Less: TDS already deducted | Credited at ITR — cash flow impact accounted. Based on ~10 trades/month at ₹20,000 notional each: 10 x 1% x ₹20,000 | (₹2,000 already deducted by exchange) |
| Less: Tax reserve (30% of gross) | Retained in system for ITR payment | (₹1,200) |
| Net profit after tax reserve | Available for distribution | ₹800 |
| Less: System retention (20% of net) | Retained for compounding buffer | (₹160) |

**Note: **The tax reserve is retained inside the Delta Exchange account and not traded with. The ₹1,200 tax reserve is held as a precautionary buffer — at ITR filing, combined TDS credit plus reserve ensures the full liability is always covered regardless of the month's trading frequency.

# **18. System Architecture**

The system uses a Sequential Modular Architecture with 11 clearly defined modules for the income phase. Each module has a single responsibility, defined inputs, defined outputs, and can be independently tested, replaced, or upgraded. This architecture evolves into a true Multi-Agent System in the compound phase when self-improvement mechanisms are added.

## **18.1 Architecture Philosophy**

| **Aspect** | **Income Phase (Sequential Modular)** | **Compound Phase (Multi-Agent)** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Role separation | 11 modules with single responsibilities | Same roles — promoted to autonomous agents |
| Communication | Structured handoffs — defined input/output contracts | Autonomous — agents decide when to communicate |
| Self-improvement | Not active — stable baseline required first | Active — agents refine parameters from outcomes |
| Orchestration complexity | Low — sequential pipeline | Higher — agent coordination layer added |
| Build time | Shorter — income phase priority | Longer — compound phase enhancement |
| Debugging | Easier — clear failure point in pipeline | More complex — requires agent-level logging |
| VPS requirements | Current ₹415 VPS sufficient | May require upgrade for parallel agent compute |

# **19. Human Override Protocol**

## **19.1 Forced Override Triggers**

The following events cause the system to halt completely and wait indefinitely for human intervention. No trading activity occurs during a forced override. If no human response is received within 24 hours, the system closes all open positions at market and remains halted.

| **#** | **Trigger** | **Reason for Forced Override** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Red Alert — 15% drawdown | Capital protection — maximum documented loss level reached |
| 2 | 3 consecutive losing trades | Signal quality review — pattern suggests model degradation or adverse regime |
| 3 | API connection failure after single retry | System cannot operate without confirmed exchange connectivity |
| 4 | Kronos model output anomaly | Nonsensical, extreme, or statistically impossible forecasts detected |
| 5 | Exchange maintenance or circuit breaker | Market integrity compromised — unknown execution environment |
| 6 | TDS deductions exceeding monthly budget projection | Tax cash flow risk — operating reserve at risk |
| 7 | Win rate below 50% for 30 consecutive days | Systematic signal failure — Kronos not performing above random |
| 8 | Any unhandled system exception | Unknown failure state — safest response is full halt |

## **19.2 Optional Override Triggers**

The following events alert the human but the system continues operating autonomously unless the human intervenes within 2 hours.

| **Trigger** | **System Default Action If No Human Response** |
| --- | --- |
| Yellow Alert — 5% drawdown | Already reduced position sizes by 50% and blocked Slot 3 — continues |
| Orange Alert — 10% drawdown | Already closed Slot 3 and halted new entries — continues with BTC+ETH monitoring only |
| Funding rate approaching 0.1% threshold | Monitoring — exits automatically if threshold breached before human responds |
| Macro event within 4 hours | Holding new entries — resumes automatically after event window passes |
| Single trade stop loss hit | Continues normally — no new entry on that asset for 4 hours |
| Win rate below 55% for 7 days (not yet 30 days) | Continues but confidence threshold temporarily raised by 5 percentage points absolute (e.g. 0.60 → 0.65). **Implementation:** Module 5 computes rolling 7-day win rate from the `trades` table at the start of each cron cycle. Requires at least 1 closed trade with `exit_timestamp` in the 7-day window (0 trades = no data = no adjustment). If win rate < 55%, `_win_rate_adjustment = 0.05` is applied additive before the extreme funding multiplier in `_check_confidence`. The cap applies in all phases including pre-live paper trading. A `win_rate_alert_7d` warning event is written per cycle when active, consumed by Module 10 (Notification). |

## **20.2 Confidence Score Threshold**

The minimum confidence threshold is NOT hardcoded at documentation stage. It is calibrated empirically during the pre-live phase and documented before live trading begins. This produces a threshold grounded in actual signal distribution data rather than theoretical assumption.

| **Period** | **Threshold Approach** | **Rationale** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pre-live weeks 1-6 | No threshold — all signals generated and logged with confidence scores | Build calibration dataset across all confidence levels |
| End of pre-live | Analyse win rate vs confidence score correlation from paper trades | Find the confidence level above which win rate consistently exceeds 55% |
| Live trading start | Threshold set at calibrated level and documented | Evidence-based threshold — not arbitrary |
| Ongoing | Threshold reviewed monthly against rolling win rate data | Adapts to model drift or regime changes |

## **20.3 Signal Performance Monitoring**

| **Metric** | **Tracking Method** | **Alert Threshold** | **Action on Breach** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Win rate per asset | Rolling 30-day calculation per asset | Below 50% | Forced override — halt signals for that asset |
| Win rate per confidence band | Weekly segmentation analysis | Any band below 45% | Exclude that confidence band from valid signals |
| Average forecast accuracy | Predicted direction vs actual close price | Degrading trend over 30 days vs baseline | Review alert to human |
| Signal frequency | Signals generated per week per asset | Drop below 3/week or spike above 20/week | Data pipeline integrity check triggered |
| Confidence score distribution | Weekly histogram analysis | Statistical shift from pre-live baseline | Potential model drift flagged |

## **20.4 Model Drift Detection**

Model drift occurs when the Kronos pretrained model loses predictive accuracy as market conditions evolve beyond its training distribution. The following signals indicate drift:

| **Drift Signal** | **Detection Method** | **Response** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Win rate declining over 60 days | Rolling win rate vs 30-day moving average | Signal review alert. If unresolved — confidence threshold raised. |
| Confidence high but outcomes poor | Correlation analysis: confidence score vs actual P&L | Confidence threshold raised. Review asset-level exclusions. |
| Forecast accuracy systematically degrading | Predicted direction accuracy vs 30-day baseline | Human review alert. Consider early fine-tuning (if in compound phase). |
| Sharp market regime change | Volatility regime detector in Data Collection module | Temporarily raise confidence threshold. Monitor closely. |

# **22. Key Terms ****&**** Glossary**

| Kronos | AI model trained on OHLCV candlestick data from 45+ exchanges to forecast future price sequences. Accepted at AAAI 2026. |
| Perpetual futures | Derivative contract with no expiry date. Positions held indefinitely until closed. Funding rate paid every 8 hours to maintain price alignment with spot market. |
| TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) | 1% deducted by exchange automatically on every cryptocurrency sell transaction above ₹10,000. Credited against tax liability at ITR filing. |

| Schedule VDA | Section of India's ITR (Income Tax Return) where all crypto trading activity must be reported annually. |

| IMPS | Immediate Payment Service. Bank transfer method supporting instant INR transfers. Used for Delta Exchange deposits. |
| Funding rate | Periodic payment between long and short position holders on perpetual futures. Positive rate: longs pay shorts. Negative rate: shorts pay longs. |
| Swing trading | Trading style holding positions for hours to days (4H to 5 days in this system), as opposed to intraday (minutes to hours) or positional (weeks to months). |
| Drawdown | Percentage decline in portfolio value from its peak. Red Alert triggers at 15% drawdown. |
| Confidence threshold | Minimum Kronos signal confidence score required before a signal is approved for execution. Calibrated empirically during pre-live. |
| Model drift | Gradual degradation in model accuracy as live market conditions evolve beyond the model's training distribution. |
| Recuperation ledger | Running tracker of cumulative human withdrawals vs ₹1,00,000 starting capital. Triggers phase transition at 100%. |
| Forced override | System halt requiring mandatory human review and decision before trading resumes. 24-hour response window. |
| Correlation regime | Classification of how closely two assets are moving together (rolling 7-day Pearson correlation). Above 0.85 = same-direction positions blocked. |
| Tax reserve | 30% of gross monthly profit retained inside Delta Exchange account — not withdrawn. Reserved for annual ITR tax payment. |
